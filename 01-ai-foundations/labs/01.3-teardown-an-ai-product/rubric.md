# Teardown Self-Assessment Rubric

Score each 1–5. Target: average ≥ 3.5, nothing below 2.

| Dimension | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **Evidence discipline** | Claims with no evidence | Most inferences cite a signal | Every inference cites evidence + calibrated confidence |
| **Stack coverage** | Only 1–2 layers | Most layers addressed | All 7 layers reasoned about |
| **Diagram quality** | One vague box | Components + flow shown | Clear, labeled, matches inferences |
| **Cost model** | Absent | Rough number, few assumptions | Quantified with explicit assumptions + arithmetic |
| **Failure analysis** | None | 1–2 failure modes | 3+ with blast radius + mitigation |
| **Confidence calibration** | Over/under-claims | Mostly reasonable | Confidence tracks evidence strength |

## Common ways to lose points
- Marking everything "High confidence".
- Skipping the cost model because "I don't know their numbers" — assumptions are the point.
- A diagram that doesn't match the inference table.
- Ignoring failure/scaling (the part interviewers probe hardest).
